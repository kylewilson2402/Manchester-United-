Landmark Seasons

The Treble Year — 1999

The 1998–99 season stands as the greatest single campaign in Manchester United's history. Under Sir Alex Ferguson, United won the Premier League on the final day of the season, beat Newcastle United in the FA Cup Final, and then, in the most dramatic fashion imaginable, came back from 1–0 down in stoppage time against Bayern Munich at Camp Nou to win the Champions League with two goals in injury time. Teddy Sheringham and Ole Gunnar Solskjær — both substitutes — scored the goals. The entire football world stood in disbelief.
