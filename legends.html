The Greats

Icons of Old Trafford

Winger

George Best

1963 – 1974  |  470 Apps  |  179 Goals

Arguably the most gifted footballer to ever pull on the red shirt, George Best arrived from Belfast as a teenager and became a global superstar. Blessed with breathtaking dribbling ability, vision, and balance, Best was the heartbeat of United's 1968 European Cup winning side and European Footballer of the Year.

Midfielder

Bobby Charlton

1956 – 1973  |  758 Apps  |  249 Goals

Sir Bobby Charlton survived the Munich Air Disaster in 1958 and went on to become one of United's greatest ever players. A World Cup winner with England in 1966 and a European Cup champion in 1968, Charlton's thunderous shooting and composure made him one of football's true legends.

Forward

Eric Cantona

1992 – 1997  |  185 Apps  |  82 Goals

King Eric. No player transformed Manchester United quite like Eric Cantona. Signed from bitter rivals Leeds United in 1992, the Frenchman was the catalyst for United's Premier League dynasty. Four league titles in five years. The popped collar. The imperious stare. A cultural icon who turned Old Trafford into his stage.

Midfielder

Roy Keane

1993 – 2005  |  480 Apps  |  51 Goals

Ferocious, relentless, and utterly uncompromising — Roy Keane was the engine room of Manchester United's most successful era. As captain, he dragged United to multiple league titles and a Champions League triumph through sheer force of will. His 1999 semi-final display against Juventus is the stuff of legend.

Forward

Cristiano Ronaldo

2003–2009, 2021–2023  |  346 Apps  |  145 Goals

Signed as a raw 18-year-old from Sporting CP, Ronaldo developed into the best player in the world at Old Trafford. Under Ferguson he won three Premier League titles, the 2008 Champions League, and the Ballon d'Or — transforming from a flashy winger into the most complete forward in football.
